## Digital business card

This was my first time using html and css, with this primarily being learned in Web Typography class.

## Fonts

Quokka, Argent Pixel 

## Process

My main idea going into this was to expand on the theming of The Pipeworks, and what little I had made so far. The mockups I made were in figma, where I tested out the different colors and shape language I would use. I chose fonts like Quokka because I wanted to lean into a hybrid of modern and also early web styling, where the web pages would sometimes represent a location that you could click around. So when I placed everything in its supposed place and marked up what different assets would be in the code, it looked very illustrative. Coding itself was mostly just setting up all the assets correctly. Getting the right size, hover effects, links, custom cursor, etc. The grid making was at the end, that way I could see all the objects in relation to one another, and I would say it looks pretty good.

## Reflection

If someone like my dad can casually peruse the site without issue or confusion, then It can be
safely assumed that people actively seeking design services out would have an even easier time
acquiring me and my services. The design tends to draw people towards the very stood out
buttons and card due to their size and placement in the site. Even if they somehow fail to notice
an asset, they won't miss another, which will inevitably lead them back to my portfolio site.​ Of course, accessibility was addressed by having every interactable have a hover effect, that way
there is no confusion on what can be clicked and what can't be clicked. Meaning a user will
potentially find their way to me accidentally.​ The only thing I would hope to improve is more user friendly views for other devices like mobile,
as many people use their phone to look at the web. Luckily this can be done on my own time.
